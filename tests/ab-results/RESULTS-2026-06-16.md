## Honest limitations (do NOT over-claim)

1. **Mechanism-level, not full pipeline.** The verify-chain (QA-equivalent) was isolated under ON/OFF — cleaner signal than full-`/harness` noise, but not the literal §7 "run `/harness` 30–40×" protocol.
2. **Small n.** 4 valid defective in-author fixtures. Margin 6 clears the pre-registered threshold, but the sample is small.
3. **fixture-02 was not pure runtime-only.** Its defect (`.hidden` vs `.hide` class mismatch) is statically visible in the source, so OFF caught it too. It is a weak render-leak fixture and should be re-seeded with a truly render-only symptom.
4. **fixture-03 build-fidelity failure (excluded).** The builder did not realize the seeded grid overflow strongly enough (3px, not ~70px). ON did not *miss* a defect — there was no defect present. Rebuild with a stronger overflow.
5. **held-out is INCONCLUSIVE and same-author.** The held-out fixtures (03, null-02) are stubs authored by the same author as the scorer. Per design §3, a real held-out split needs an **independent author**. Generalization is not yet established.
6. **M4 (untestable discipline) not run.** This pass covered only M1 (render-leak). `untestable.csv` remains header-only.

## What this does and does not justify

- **Does:** record the first real, positive, pre-registered A/B data point for observation-grounding (in-author KEEP, 0 FP).
- **Does NOT yet:** justify flipping the CHANGELOG "effect unmeasured" → "validated". Per design, a status flip requires reviewing the **held-out** split (currently INCONCLUSIVE) and recording model+effort. Next step: independent-author held-out fixtures + fixture-03 rebuild + M4.
